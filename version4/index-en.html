Abstract

Working alongside our industry collaborators from IRMAI (https://irmai.io), we address the multi-billion dollar annual drain on the financial sector caused by operational risks losses fueled by fragmented systems and disconnected data silos. In response, we have developed a specialised ontology for financial operational risks, with a targeted focus on mortgages and FX stocks. This ontology acts as a schema for a multimodal knowledge-graph-based digital twin that aims to enable monitoring of risks in complex financial environments.
This research is a key output of the project "Investigating Multimodal Knowledge Graphs as Infrastructures of Digital Twins of Financial Risks," funded by the UKFin+ network.
A short video summarising the project (and the use of the ontology) is available online (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=p3aTl0Ts9HM).

Financial Risk and Mortgage Ontology: Description back to ToC

Financial Digital Twin Ontology (financialDT) is a proof-of-concept ontology developed to investigate the use of semantic technologies in modelling financial operational risk through multi-modal data integration. The ontology provides a formal conceptual framework for constructing knowledge graphs capable of representing financial institutional structures, product lifecycles, and risk dynamics in a unified semantic model. The ontology adopts a layered modular architecture comprising three principal layers: the Financial Institutional Layer (FIL), the Risk Layer, and the Financial Product Layer. The Financial Institutional Layer is conceptually aligned with principles from FIBO and models the core organisational, contractual, and account-level semantics required to represent financial institutions and their operational structure. The Risk Layer is inspired by ISO 31000 and provides a structured representation of risk sources, risk events, exposures, assessments, mitigation strategies, and control actions. It enables explicit modelling of risk identification, analysis, evaluation, and treatment within a financial digital twin context. The Financial Product Layer captures the semantics necessary to represent financial products and their lifecycle states. Mortgages are used as a proof-of-concept instantiation to demonstrate how product structures interact with institutional entities and risk mechanisms. The layered modular structure allows individual components of the ontology to be reused independently or integrated selectively, supporting interoperability with institution-specific organisational models and product definitions while preserving a coherent global risk reasoning framework.
